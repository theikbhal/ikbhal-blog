## Morning Ideas, Holding Hands

This morning I woke up with three ideas.

Not fully formed. Not written down. Just there — floating in that half-asleep space where everything feels possible.

I've learned the hard way: if I don't capture them immediately, they're gone by breakfast.

### The ADHD Morning Flood

Ideas hit hardest in the morning for me. Right after waking up, before the noise of the day starts. No notifications, no context switches, just raw thoughts.

New app features. Blog post angles. Improvements to existing projects. Random thoughts about how to solve a problem I was stuck on the night before.

Three, four, five at a time. All holding hands, tumbling over each other.

### The Capture Ritual

I keep my phone on the nightstand. First thing I do — grab it, open a blank note, dump everything. No editing. No judging. Just words on a page.

- "Little Wins — add streak counter, visually warm, not gamified"
- "Blog post about Flutter vs Expo — write the comparison table"
- "Home button on blog — 🏠, works from any page"

Most of these will never ship. That's fine. The act of writing them down does two things:

1. **Clears the mental cache** — gets them out of my head so I can focus on the day
2. **Seeds the backlog** — when I sit down to code, I have a list to choose from

### The Ones That Stick

Some ideas fade. Some stay. The ones that keep coming back, days later — those are worth building.

The 🏠 home button I shipped today started as a morning scribble three days ago. Every time I opened my own blog on mobile and had to scroll back up to tap "ikbhal", the idea came back. Eventually I just built it.

### Holding Hands Builds Momentum

When ideas hold hands — when one leads to another and they form a chain — that's when progress happens.

Morning → Capture → Backlog → Build → Ship

One little win at a time.

---

*Morning ideas. Hold them close.*
