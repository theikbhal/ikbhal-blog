## The Daily School Run — My Real Routine

Before any code ships, before any blog post goes live, this is what my day looks like.

### Morning

- **8:30 AM** — Drop elder son at college
- **9:00 AM** — Drop younger kids at school
- After drop — Ro water shop, fill the water can

### Afternoon

- **12:20 PM** — Pick up kids from school (first batch)
- **1:20 PM** — Drop them back (second session)
- Between runs — help kids get ready, fill water bottles, prep bags

### Evening

- **4:25 PM** — Pick up kids from school (final)
- **5:30 PM** — Pick up elder son from college

### The Reality

That's **8 trips** a day. In between: water cans, uniforms, homework, bottles, snacks.

Everything I build — the blog posts, the Little Wins app, the code — happens in the gaps between these trips. A 40-minute window here. An hour after Isha there.

Building in public doesn't mean I have endless time. It means I use what I have.

### Why I Write This

Not for sympathy. Just context. If you're reading this and you also build things in small pockets of time between responsibilities — you're not alone.

One trip. One commit. One little win at a time.

---

*The school run is the container. The code is what fills it.*
