## Self-Diagnosed, Assuming ADHD

I've never been formally diagnosed. But the pattern is hard to ignore:

- Get frustrated easily
- Start things, don't finish them
- Can't focus on one thing for long
- Feel like I'm not achieving anything
- Brain always jumping to the next thing before the current one is done

I'm not looking for a label. I'm looking for what works.

Here's the toolkit I've assembled so far.

## Pomodoro — 15/3

Standard pomodoros (25/5) are too long. My focus window is about 15 minutes. So I do 15 minutes of work, 3 minutes break. Sometimes 4 rounds, sometimes 2. The short window makes starting easy.

Using Pomodoro on macOS (the menu bar app).

## XMind — Mind Maps

When my brain is scattered, I open XMind and dump everything into a mind map. No structure, no hierarchy at first. Just get it out. Then I organize.

It's the fastest way for me to go from "too many thoughts" to "okay, here's the plan."

## Obsidian — Daily Notes

My second brain. I have a daily note template that opens every morning with:

- What's the one thing I want to get done today?
- Brain dump (uncensored)
- Wins from yesterday
- Links to active projects

The daily note is the anchor. Everything else branches from it.

## Hand-Held Writing

Recently started writing by hand more. There's something about pen on paper that forces a different kind of thinking. Slower. More deliberate. Less skimming.

I use it when I'm truly stuck — when typing feels like I'm just rearranging the same thoughts.

## Stickies

macOS Stickies app is underrated. I keep 3-4 stickies on my desktop at all times:

1. Today's focus (one line)
2. Brain dump (random thoughts to process later)
3. Wins log (small things I achieved)
4. Currently blocked (things I'm stuck on)

When a sticky gets too full, I move it to Obsidian.

## Brain Dump

Before I can focus on anything, I need to empty my head. I do a brain dump into:

- Obsidian daily note (morning)
- A sticky (during the day)
- Interstitial journal (between tasks)

The goal is simple: get it out of my head so it stops looping.

## Interstitial Journal — Just One Line

The interstitial journal is the most recent addition. Between every context switch — before pomodoro, after pomodoro, before a meeting, after a meeting — I write one line:

- What I just did
- What I'm about to do

That's it. One line. It prevents the "what was I doing?" loop that costs me 10 minutes every time I get distracted.

The app I built for this is at `interstitial-journal/` in my workspace. Simple web app. One page, one line.

## How It All Fits Together

```
Morning → Obsidian daily note + brain dump
During day → 15/3 pomodoros + interstitial journal between each
Scattered → XMind mind map
Stuck → Hand-write it out
Need reminder → Stickies on desktop
End of day → Log wins in daily note
```

It's not perfect. I still get frustrated, still fail to focus, still start things I don't finish. But the gaps between those moments are getting smaller.

That's progress.
