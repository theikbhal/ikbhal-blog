## Experimenting: Canva, VN Editor, and the CapCut That Wasn't There

Goal: **10 reels today**. ADHD brain needs the path of least resistance.

### The Search

Went to Mac App Store (India) looking for CapCut. It's not there. Maybe region-locked, maybe shadow-banned in India App Store. Either way — dead end.

So I tried what was available.

### Experiment 1: Canva Mac App

Canva is on the App Store. Free. Opened it.

**What's good:**
- 9:16 preset in one click
- Drag-and-drop, no timeline overwhelm
- Reel templates ready to use
- Everything feels familiar

**What's not:**
- Feels like a design tool trying to be a video editor
- Export takes longer than it should
- Free version limits some videos features
- Heavy app — Mac Mini fan spun up

**Verdict:** Works for quick template-based reels. Not for 10 in a row.

### Experiment 2: VN Video Editor

Also on App Store. Free. No watermark. Interesting.

**What's good:**
- Real video editor, not a design tool
- 9:16 preset in New Project
- No watermark on export (rare for free)
- Multi-track timeline but not overwhelming
- Auto captions, speed curves, keyframe — all free
- 4K 60fps export

**What's not:**
- Slightly more steps than CapCut
- UI is functional, not pretty
- Takes a minute to learn where things are

**Verdict:** The best free option on India App Store. One extra click vs CapCut.

### Experiment 3: CapCut from Website

Searching the India Mac App Store? Not there. But capcut.com has a direct DMG download. Works on Mac. Same app.

So the workaround: download from capcut.com, not the store.

### The ADHD Problem

The real issue isn't the tool. It's me:

- Download Canva → open → close after 2 mins
- Download VN → open → feels unfamiliar → close
- Search for CapCut → not found → give up for 20 mins
- Finally download from website → it works → why didn't I do this first

The ADHD tax: 45 minutes finding a tool, 10 minutes using it.

### What I'm Actually Using

**VN Video Editor** for today. It's free, no watermark, 9:16 works. One reel in ~5 mins. 10 reels = done by lunch if I stop switching tools.
