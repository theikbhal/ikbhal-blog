## The Weekly Vegetable Run — And Other Things I Drop

### The Routine

Once a week, I'm supposed to go to the market and buy vegetables for the week.

Chicken or mutton — if there's money left after everything else.

Sunday — at least one trip to the park with the family. My wife pushes for it. I know it's good for everyone. But the learning startup (this build-in-public thing, the app, the blog) always feels more urgent.

### What Actually Happens

Some weeks I go. Most weeks I don't.

- I forget. Plain forget. The day passes and I realize at 9 PM the market is closed.
- I'm exhausted. The school runs, the mental load, the feeling that I'm not moving fast enough on anything.
- Depression creeps in. Days where getting out of bed feels like a win. The market doesn't stand a chance.
- No goal achieved. When I haven't shipped anything, when the week feels empty, I don't feel like I deserve to do normal things.
- Sometimes I just... don't go. No deep reason. The thought of the crowded market, the bargaining, the carrying bags — it's too much.

### The Workaround

My elder son buys from the local shop when I miss the market. Not the same quality. Not the same price. But it's something.

### Sunday Meat

Sunday is meat day in our house. But I miss it often.

Same reasons. Exhaustion. Depression. Forgetting. Feeling like I didn't accomplish enough to justify the expense.

My wife doesn't complain. That makes it worse somehow.

### Why I'm Writing This

Because building in public isn't just about the code. It's about the person writing the code. And the person writing the code sometimes forgets to buy vegetables.

I don't have a solution yet. Maybe writing it down helps me remember next week. Maybe being honest about it makes it easier to fix.

Maybe next Sunday I'll go to the park.

*The small things are the real things.*
