## Why CapCut — And Why Mac Mini for Reels

### The Requirement

I need **10 reels today**. Target: **50 DMs**. That's a lot of content.

On mobile, making reels is easy — record in the app, upload, done. But 10 reels on a phone? Clunky. Battery drains. Screen too small to edit properly. And I get distracted by notifications every 30 seconds.

My requirements for the tool:

- **Free** — no subscriptions
- **Fast** — ADHD can't wait for renders
- **9:16 vertical** — one click, not manual math
- **Simple UI** — no timeline overwhelm
- **Works on Mac Mini** — big screen, no distractions

### Why CapCut

I tried a few:

| App | Verdict |
| --- | --- |
| iMovie | No native 9:16, fight every export |
| DaVinci Resolve | Way too heavy for quick reels |
| LazyReel | Great for auto, but I want control |
| **CapCut** | Free, fast, 9:16 in one click |

CapCut is the Instagram of video editors — low barrier, high output.

### What I Do

1. Open CapCut → New Project → **9:16** preset
2. Drag in my footage (screen recording, camera clip, whatever)
3. Trim silence from start/end
4. Auto captions (huge time save)
5. Add music from library
6. Export 1080×1920 → done

One reel takes ~3-5 minutes. 10 reels = about an hour.

### Why Not Mobile

Mobile is great for one-off posts. But 10 reels on a phone means:

- Editing on a 6-inch screen
- Battery hits 20% by reel 4
- Instagram notifications every 2 minutes
- fomo for watch ads

Mac Mini + CapCut = batch mode. Zero distractions. Full keyboard shortcuts.
