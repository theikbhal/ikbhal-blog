## Why Build in Public?

I've been building things quietly for years. Apps, websites, tools — mostly for myself, sometimes for others. But I've never shared the process.

This blog is where that changes.

### The Problem

I have ADHD. I start a lot of things. I finish some. The ones I finish usually come from:
- **Accountability** — someone expecting it
- **Momentum** — small wins stacking up
- **Simplicity** — not over-engineering before launch

Building in public gives me all three. When I know someone might read this, I'm more likely to ship. When I log small wins publicly, momentum builds. And when I keep it simple, I actually launch.

### What I'm Building

Right now I'm working on **Little Wins** — a tiny app for ADHD brains to log and celebrate small achievements. It has:

- A wins tracker with confetti celebrations
- A focus mode (one thing at a time)
- A zikir counter
- ParNotes for quick capture
- Dark mode (because of course)

The [Mac app](https://github.com/theikbhal/little-wins) is a menu bar Electron app. The [iPhone app](https://github.com/theikbhal/little-wins) is built with Expo. Both are open source.

### What You'll See Here

- Daily/weekly updates on what I'm building
- Code snippets and design decisions
- What broke and how I fixed it
- The messy reality of shipping software

No curated success stories. Just the actual process.
