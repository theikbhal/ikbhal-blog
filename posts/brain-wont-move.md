## I Don't Know What Happened to My Brain

Today's target: 9:30 AM, work on the Little Wins App Store launch.

It's now past 11. I haven't touched it.

Instead, I spent the last hour — maybe hour and a half — writing blog posts. On this blog. The ikbhal blog. The one you're reading right now.

### The Bait and Switch

The plan was clear. I knew what to do. Open Xcode. Take screenshots. Run `eas build`. Push to App Store Connect.

But when 9:30 came, I opened the blog instead. New post. Then another. Then another. Five posts in one sitting.

Each one felt productive. Writing is productive. Publishing is productive. But it wasn't the *right* productive.

### What Is This?

I don't know if it's:
- **Task aversion** — the App Store launch is big and scary and has unknown unknowns. The blog is easy and safe.
- **ADHD switching** — brain latched onto the dopamine hit of creating new posts instead of finishing the hard thing.
- **Perfectionism dressed as productivity** — "I'm doing something useful" while avoiding the main thing.
- **Burnout avoidance** — the launch requires energy I don't have right now.

Probably all of them.

### The Result

- 5 new blog posts ✅
- 0 App Store progress ❌
- 1 confused brain 🧠

### What Now

It's 11 AM. The school run is at 12:20. I have about an hour.

Maybe I open Xcode. Maybe I don't. But at least I know what happened. Writing it down draws the line between distraction and avoidance.

Tomorrow I'll try again at 9:30.

---

*The main thing is not the only thing. But it is the main thing.*
