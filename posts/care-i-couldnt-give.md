## The Care I Couldn't Give

Someone dear to me is unwell.

Tooth pain. Low blood. Tailbone problem. Other things I won't list.

They need time. They need attention. They need money for treatment.

I haven't been able to give any of it properly.

### The Weight

Every day I know I should check on them. Take them to the doctor. Sit with them. Make sure they're okay.

But between the school runs, the building, the exhaustion, the depression — I keep putting it off. Not because I don't care. Because I'm overwhelmed by the caring.

It sounds backwards. It is backwards.

### The Money

Treatment costs money. I don't have much. Every time I think about it, I feel the gap between what I should do and what I can do.

I tell myself: once the app ships, once there's income, once things settle. But health doesn't wait for settling.

### The Avoidance

I check my phone instead of checking on them. I open the code editor instead of opening the door. I tell myself I'll do it tomorrow.

Tomorrow comes. Same thing.

### Why Write This

Not for absolution. Not for advice.

Because writing it down makes it real. Stops the background guilt from being background. Puts it in front of me.

Maybe this week I'll actually go.

*Some debts can't be paid with code.*
